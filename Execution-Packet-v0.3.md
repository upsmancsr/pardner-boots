# Part 1 — Footwear Developer / Technical Design RFP

## Project Overview

We are developing an Austin-designed children's western boot built around a natural/anatomical foot shape. It should look like a real kids cowboy boot but correct the fit problems common in traditional children's western boots: narrow toe boxes, raised heels, exaggerated toe spring, and adult construction scaled down for kids.

We're looking for a footwear designer/developer to translate the founder's product vision, early 3D concept work, fit priorities, and unit economics into a manufacturer-ready package.

## Product Summary

- **Product:** children's western-style cowboy boot on a new anatomical last.
- **Founding-batch size range:** US kids **8C–12C** (full range 6C–13C later). **Sample size: 10C.**
- **Closure:** hidden inner (medial) **side zip** as the functional closure; western pull tabs retained as styling/assist.
- **Retail:** $118 (founding preorder ~$98), with headroom above $118 if warranted.
- **Launch:** one style, one color, one outsole, one MVP. **MVP color:** warm caramel/camel tan leather.
- **Priority features:** anatomical toe box · low/zero internal drop · low toe spring · secure heel fit · easy on/off · child-safe & compliance-ready · premium leather-forward look.

## Current Status

**We have:** a clear product concept; founder-defined fit priorities; an early 3D concept model (FreeCAD, built on a downloaded *anatomical* kids last, used as a communication tool only — **not** a licensing basis or production last); basic lower-boot dimensions; initial thinking on size, material, price, and MVP scope; and a firm intent to own the product design, the last, and the Austin/Texas brand origin.

**We need help with:** last strategy and creation; shaft design; stitch/pattern direction; the notched zero-drop outsole; material spec; construction; tech pack; factory communication package; sample development; landed-cost feasibility.

## Desired Partner Profile

Experience with several of: children's footwear · boots / western boots · **anatomical / barefoot / natural-foot footwear** · **last development (not just modification)** · footwear tech packs · leather footwear · overseas factory development · early-stage footwear brands · fit testing and sample iteration · manufacturing-ready documentation · cost-aware design.

We especially want a developer whose reference points are **anatomical/kids**, not a traditional western bootmaker who will instinctively pull the toe back in. This is a fit-architecture and cost-feasibility project, not a fashion-sketching one.

## Scope of Work

### Phase 1 — Diagnostic Sprint *(standalone, paid; everything after is contingent)*

A short paid engagement to pressure-test feasibility before any larger commitment. Review the Founder Product Brief, Technical Fit Brief, the 3D concept model, and visual/competitor references, then deliver a written memo covering: technical feasibility · last risks · heel-fit risks in a **zero-drop side-zip** boot · internal-drop feasibility · toe-spring feasibility · the **notched-underside outsole** approach at a **slim ≈15 mm-or-less stack** (perceived heel from notch depth, not thickness) · material and construction options · cost drivers · tooling risks (and whether the founding batch can ship on **hand/die-cut tooling** to defer the mold) · MOQ risks · design-simplification opportunities · recommended development sequence.

**Deliverable:** product-development memo with recommended next steps. We will scope Phases 2–5 *after* this.

### Phase 2 — Last & Fit Strategy

Define the requirements for a **new anatomical children's last** (or an adaptation of an existing *anatomical* last). Deliverables may include: recommendation to build/commission a clean-slate last the company owns; 10C sample-size last strategy; notes for the last maker/factory; toe-box, heel, and instep guidance; internal-drop and toe-spring requirements; fit-test plan.

**Important:** we do **not** want a standard western last widened toward anatomical. Toe shape, toe spring, and drop *are* the last; the correct direction is anatomical-first with western styling added.

### Phase 3 — MVP Design Development

Develop the MVP while preserving the founder's creative direction: side/front/back/top views · shaft shape · **hidden side-zip placement** · pull-tab styling · vamp · stitch pattern · anatomical toe direction · **outsole side profile with the notched visual heel** · material/color direction · branding placement.

**Design goal:** classic western language, softened and modern, appropriate for a child's natural foot — western carried by shaft, vamp, stitch, pull tabs, and heel profile, with a proudly anatomical toe.

### Phase 4 — Technical Package

Manufacturer-ready tech pack: product overview · size range (founding 8C–12C, sample 10C) · 2D flats · **new-last requirements** · upper pattern notes · shaft height/circumference · toe shape · **heel/drop notes (flat-top, notched-underside, zero internal drop)** · toe-spring notes · **outsole requirements incl. notch geometry and hand/die-cut vs. molded options** · insole/sockliner (removable) · lining · material specs · caramel/camel color callouts · stitching · **side-zip spec + pull-tab styling** · branding · BOM · construction notes · compliance/safety notes · packaging basics · spec sheet · initial grading guidance · sample-comments template.

### Phase 5 — Manufacturer RFQ Support

Prepare and (optionally) run the factory-facing package: product summary · technical/MOQ/sampling/material/last/compliance/FOB/landed-cost questions · recommended manufacturer profile. Optional: review responses, compare quotes, join sample reviews, mark up prototype photos, provide revision comments.

## Key Product Requirements

Must prioritize: anatomical toe box · low/zero internal drop · low toe spring · secure heel fit · easy on/off · child-safe & compliance-ready · premium western aesthetic.

Must **not** become: a narrow standard cowboy boot · a generic barefoot boot · a costume boot · a cheap synthetic boot · a stiff miniature adult boot · a product that depends on exaggerated heel height or toe spring to look western.

## Cost-Feasibility Ask *(honest costing — no target to design toward)*

We are building a premium DTC boot at **$118 retail (founding preorder ~$98), with headroom to price higher** if the product warrants it. Please **cost the product honestly at spec** — do not design to an artificial landed-cost target at the expense of fit or premium feel. Tell us:

- the realistic **landed-cost range** at a small founding-batch volume and at scale/reorder;
- the biggest **cost drivers** (last, notched outsole, leather grade, construction, stitching, lining, MOQ, compliance, duty/freight);
- the **levers** that move landed cost most, and which are safe (non-visible: lining, insole, stitch complexity, shaft material) vs. which would damage the differentiator (leather, real outsole, boot structure);
- whether the **founding batch can avoid hard outsole tooling** via CNC/die-cut hand-finished soles, deferring the mold to reorder.

## Budget Expectations (design/development stage)

Phase-based pricing with clear deliverables; separate line items for the diagnostic sprint, last creation/refinement, and tech pack; optional hourly for sampling/factory review.

Indicative ranges: diagnostic sprint **$1,500–$3,500** · lean technical/design support **$3,000–$6,000** · fuller design + tech pack + last strategy **$7,000–$12,000** · extended support through multiple samples **$12,000–$20,000+**. Cost-conscious, but not at the expense of the last, fit, manufacturability, or unit economics.

## Questions for Candidates

Children's footwear experience? · Boots / western / **side-zip** footwear? · Anatomical / barefoot / wide-toe / zero-drop? · Can you **create** (not just modify) a last? · Manufacturer-ready tech packs? · Can you advise a 10C sample-size last strategy before a full graded run? · What inputs do you need / deliverables would you provide? · Factory vs. developer responsibilities? · Biggest risks you see? · How do you keep it western while the toe stays anatomical? · **How do you secure the heel in a zero-drop side-zip boot?** · **Is our notched-underside zero-drop outsole feasible at a slim ≈15 mm-or-less stack, and can the founding batch ship on hand/die-cut tooling?** · Realistic landed-cost range and the levers? · Relevant factory relationships? · Sample review/revision support? · Estimated cost and timeline?

# Part 2 — Costed Roadmap

## Working Assumptions

Kids western-style cowboy boot on a new anatomical last · founding range **8C–12C** (full 6C–13C later), sample **10C** · warm caramel/camel tan leather · hidden side zip · retail **$118** (preorder ~$98, headroom above) · **startup budget $50,000 = validation-through-preorder** · production a separate, preorder-funded tranche · Austin-designed, expert overseas partners · one hero boot.

**Primary risks to solve, in order:** Will parents pay ~$118? · Can it look western on an anatomical last? · Can the heel stay secure with a wide toe + zero drop (now: does the side zip solve it)? · Can it be made at a landed cost that supports margin? · Can it meet children's-product compliance? · Can the first run stay small enough to avoid overbuying?

## Spending Principle — Spend in Gates

Do not spend the $50k evenly. Each phase answers one question; a negative answer means pause, revise, or stop before the next large spend. **Protect money for** last/fit validation, prototype rounds, compliance, and contingency. **Avoid early over-spend on** brand agency, **the production outsole mold**, multiple colorways, large inventory, polished packaging, expensive paid ads, and a full graded last set before fit is proven.

## Two-Tranche Budget

**Tranche 1 — Validation through preorder ($50,000).** Gets you a validated, sampled, compliance-scoped product and a live preorder. Does **not** include the production outsole mold or the first production MOQ.

| Category | Target Spend |
|---|---|
| Research, parent interviews, competitor samples | $2,000 |
| Logo / visual basics | $1,000 |
| Landing page / ecommerce setup | $500 |
| Concept renders (AI now + commissioned later) | $1,000 |
| Footwear diagnostic sprint | $3,000 |
| Design / new-last / tech-pack development | $12,000 |
| Manufacturer sourcing / RFQ support | $1,500 |
| Prototype / sample rounds (**4–6**, hand/die-cut soles) | $15,000 |
| Compliance / testing reserve (initial) | $5,000 |
| Photography / content / preorder launch | $3,500 |
| Contingency | $5,500 |
| **Tranche 1 total** | **~$50,000** |

**Tranche 2 — Production (separate; preorder-funded + additional capital as needed).** Firm these numbers once the diagnostic and RFQ return real MOQ/FOB figures.

| Category | Indicative |
|---|---|
| Outsole tooling | **$0 for founding batch if deferred** (hand/die-cut); ~$8,000–$20,000+ when molded at reorder |
| First production run (MOQ × landed) | firm at RFQ (e.g., 300 pairs × ~$45–$60 landed) |
| Freight, duty, inspection | $4,000–$7,000 |
| **Funded by** | preorder revenue (e.g., 100–300 × $98 = $10k–$30k) **plus** an additional capital tranche |

> Reality check: $50k does **not** carry you through tooling + first production. It carries you beautifully through a validated sample and a preorder launch. Production is a deliberate second gate, funded largely by demand you've already proven.

## The Landed-Cost Lever *(framework now; decision later, with real quotes)*

When a quote comes back above target, pull levers in this order:
1. **Raise retail** ($118 → $128–$138) — the only lever that doesn't damage the premium justification, and our audience supports it.
2. **Simplify non-visible cost** — lining, insole, stitch complexity, shaft material. Never cut the visible/structural differentiators (leather, real outsole, boot structure).
3. **Accept thin founding-batch margin** — deliberate and temporary, to buy proof; never steady-state.

Underneath all three: price around the **scaled/reorder** cost (tooling amortizes, MOQs gain leverage), not the scary run-#1 number. **Dedicated session once the diagnostic sprint returns real quotes.**

## Phase 0 — Organize the Business Hypothesis *(1 week · $0–$500)*

Master folder: Founder Product Brief · Technical Fit Brief · Developer RFP · current 3D model · sketches · moodboard · competitor screenshots · budget sheet. Lock MVP direction (caramel leather, 8C–12C founding / 10C sample, $118 / ~$98, side zip, new anatomical last). Build a competitor grid (brand · product · price · material · toe shape · drop/heel · shaft · closure · sizes · reviews · complaints · what customers love).

**Gate:** you can state the product in one sentence — *"A real cowboy boot — built like the grown-up ones, shaped like a kid's foot."*

## Phase 1 — Competitive Research & Demand Signals *(2–3 wks · $1,000–$3,500)* — start now, in parallel

1. **Inspect competitors** — Tecovas/Ariat/Old West/Roper kids western; Little Love Bug (soft-sole western, ~$60 — the price anchor to beat on "real boot"); Ten Little, See Kai Run; barefoot kids boots (Xero/Feelgrounds); adult barefoot-western (Shapen, Koel) for the side-zip precedent. Measure toe width/taper, heel/drop, toe spring, shaft, entry, sole stiffness, leather quality, weight, packaging.
2. **15–25 parent interviews** — Austin/Texas parents, kids-cowboy-boot buyers, barefoot/wide-toe buyers, $80+ kids-shoe spenders, sizes in range. Test which line lands: *"barefoot cowboy boot"* vs. *"a real cowboy boot, built for kids' growing feet."* Probe $98 / $118 / what makes it worth it / would they preorder from a real sample.
3. **Minimal waitlist page (launch this week).** Don't let render polish block it — generate a few **AI concept renders** now (caramel kids western boot, broad anatomical toe, low stacked-look heel, side zip), label them "concept," and test message + price. Commission a proper render from the diagnostic developer once engaged (reused in the RFP and the preorder page — pay once, use three times). Headline: *"Real cowboy boots. For real little feet."*

**Gate:** continue if ≥2 hold — parents understand the problem · dislike existing fit · $98 feels easy to a meaningful group · $118 acceptable if premium · they ask when they can buy · waitlist conversion justifies sample spend.

## Phase 2 — Diagnostic Sprint *(source 1–2 wks, work 1–2 wks · $1,500–$3,500)*

Source 10–15 candidates (footwear/kids/barefoot developers, last makers). Send the short RFP; hire a **paid diagnostic sprint, not a full engagement.** Require written answers on: is our concept last usable as reference · does western conflict with the anatomical toe · is zero drop realistic · **does the notched-underside outsole work** · **can the founding batch ship on hand/die-cut soles to defer the mold** · realistic landed-cost range · factory vs. developer split. Finalist questions: heel security without narrowing the toe; western visual heel without internal rise; what to simplify; what pushes landed cost up; where founders waste money.

**Gate:** ≥1 credible developer believes it's feasible, explains the last/heel/on-off tradeoff clearly, works in phases, understands the economics, and has taste you trust for the western read.

## Phase 3 — Last Strategy & MVP Design *(3–6 wks · $8,000–$14,000)*

**You own:** taste, western direction, caramel target, brand language, fit hierarchy, silhouette approval. **Developer owns:** last creation/plan, flats, shaft, pattern/construction feasibility, outsole recommendation, tech-pack structure, 10C sample-last guidance.

Create 2–3 directions (classic / modern-minimal / heritage-premium); pick one. Build **one new 10C anatomical sample last** (current model = concept only; company owns the new last). Decide first-sample material (all-leather vs. leather vamp + cost-conscious shaft; corrected-grain / soft cowhide / goat). Confirm **side zip + pull-tab styling.** Keep the founding batch on **hand/die-cut soles** unless the developer shows a stock option meets the notch/drop concept.

**Gate:** still reads western · toe still visibly anatomical · plausible heel-fit strategy · no mold required pre-validation · estimated landed cost still supports $118+.

## Phase 4 — Manufacturer Sourcing & RFQ *(3–6 wks · $1,000–$3,000)*

The ideal factory may be a **kids or barefoot footwear factory that can execute western styling** — not a classic western boot factory. Build ~20 candidates across kids / leather-boot / barefoot / western / low-MOQ boutique. Send a controlled RFQ (summary · Technical Fit Brief · flats · material target · 8C–12C founding range / 10C sample · retail $118 · **honest-costing ask, no fixed target** · questions on new-last capability, zero-drop/notched outsole, low toe spring, hand/die-cut vs. molded soles, MOQ, sample cost/rounds, caramel leather sourcing, test reports, US kids compliance history, **who owns the last/patterns**, pre-shipment inspection, FOB range, what pushes landed cost up).

**Gate:** ≥2 credible factories can sample · ≥1 supports the new-last requirement · MOQ survivable · sample costs in plan · estimated landed cost supports $118+.

## Phase 5 — Prototype Round 1 *(4–8 wks after kickoff · $4,000–$7,000; $7,000–$10,000 if two factories)*

Approve the 10C last direction; confirm sample spec (10C · caramel · one outsole · **side zip** · one stitch · one shaft). Require the factory to document internal drop, toe spring, last dimensions, materials, deviations, FOB/landed implications. Review the sample: reads western? toe box right? **drop actually zero/near-zero?** toe spring low? material worth $118+? zip easy? heel slip? sole too sneaker-like? Photograph/video on and off foot; fit-test on 5–8 children.

**Gate:** concept works physically · parents react to the look · revisable without major re-engineering · last directionally correct · material in range · landed cost still plausible.

## Phase 6 — Prototype Round 2+ & Fit Validation *(4–8 wks · $4,000–$7,000; plan for 4–6 rounds total)*

Revise last, heel fit, shaft, zip/entry opening, toe shape/spring, outsole/drop, material, stitch. Fit-test 15–25 children/parents with a structured survey (size, width, instep, current brand, entry ease, heel slip, toe room, walking comfort, aesthetic rating, perceived quality, $98/$118 intent, preorder intent, blockers). Capture on-child, walking, zip-entry, and toe-box comparison media. Validate **8C and 12C** before grading the founding run.

**Gate:** fit issues manageable · parents get it quickly · $118+ still plausible · photographs well · enough preorder intent · factory can quote production with acceptable MOQ/timeline.

## Phase 7 — Compliance & Production Readiness *(2–6 wks · $4,000–$7,000 initial)*

Identify applicable rules with a compliance consultant/lab. Get factory documentation (leather/dye/adhesive/trim reports, prior kids-footwear compliance). Remove risk (no detachable charms, no unnecessary metal, no untested coatings). Define tracking-label placement; assign responsibility for testing, CPC, records, import docs, inspection. Remember **leather dyes/tanning = lead/chromium** — test the caramel early.

**Gate:** testing/compliance path clear · factory can document · no red flags · you understand cost/timeline before promising preorder ship dates.

## Phase 8 — Preorder / Founding Batch Launch *(2–4 wks prep + 2–4 wks campaign · $2,500–$5,000)*

Build the product page internally (founder story · Austin-designed · fit philosophy · size guide · real sample photos · toe-box comparison · materials · ship window · refund policy · FAQ). Launch to the waitlist, Austin/Texas parent groups, western-lifestyle and barefoot/natural-foot communities, and your social audience. Offer ~$98 founding price, limited first run, clear ship date, refundable deposit structure. Track signup/ATC/preorder conversion, size demand, price objections, and which message converts (western style vs. foot health vs. Austin-designed).

**Preorder gate before placing production:** strong = 100+ paid preorders/deposits · moderate = 50+ paid + strong waitlist · early = 300–500 qualified emails with clear size demand. A large *unpaid* waitlist is not enough — footwear sizing uncertainty makes paid intent the real signal.

## Phase 9 — First Production Run *(Tranche 2 · factory-dependent · $10,000–$25,000+, preorder-funded + additional capital)*

Use preorder size data to set the curve. Keep it simple (one color, one style, founding sizes). Now decide **hand/die-cut vs. cutting the mold** based on run size. Negotiate MOQ, deposit %, balance timing, inspection rights, defect policy, substitution approval, **last/pattern ownership**, reorder timeline, packaging. Arrange inspection, freight, duty, receiving, fulfillment, and support tooling (fit guide, exchange policy, care instructions, "how to measure your child's foot," wide-toe/zero-drop/western-fit FAQ).

## Founder Operating Principle

Not "make the perfect boot in isolation" — **learn, in the right order:** Does the customer care? → Can the last work? → Can it look right? → Can the factory make it? → Can the landed cost work? → Can it be safely sold? → Can the first batch sell before cash runs out? **Every dollar answers one of those questions.**
