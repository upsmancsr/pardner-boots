# Founder Product Brief — v0.3
### Kids Anatomical Western Boot

> Changes from v0.2: new anatomical last (never a modified western last); inner (medial) side zip, exposed (no flap) as the primary closure with pull tabs kept as styling; founding-batch size range narrowed to 8C–12C (6C–13C as post-validation expansion); positioning spine and beachhead sharpened; landed-cost target softened to internal-only (not a vendor anchor); outsole tooling reframed from "avoid" to "sequence last."

---

## Working Product Thesis

**A real cowboy boot, designed for kids' growing feet.**

This product exists because traditional kids western boots are usually scaled-down adult boots: narrow toe boxes, raised heels, unnecessary toe spring, stiff construction, and styling that prioritizes the western silhouette over the actual shape of a child's foot.

Our boot keeps the visual language of a classic western boot but rebuilds the foundation around a child's natural foot shape.

## Positioning Spine (the one sentence everything hangs on)

**A real cowboy boot — built like the grown-up ones, shaped like a kid's foot.**

This carries quality, ruggedness, and versatility implicitly (real boots are all of those) and pins our single differentiator — *fit* — to our category anchor — *real boot*. Our nearest kids competitor is a soft-sole toddler bootie styled western. We are the opposite: a legitimately constructed boot that also happens to be built correctly for a child's foot.

**Beachhead:** lead with western / quality-conscious parents who already spend on real boots, and use fit as the closer. The barefoot / natural-foot community is a strong *second* audience (they'll celebrate the anatomical toe), but western-first framing is the wedge — we are not a barefoot shoe with western garnish.

## Brand Origin

Founded and designed in Austin, Texas. Texas-rooted and founder-led, with design direction, product standards, fit philosophy, and customer experience owned by the company, even though manufacturing runs through expert footwear partners overseas.

**Preferred language:** Austin-designed · Texas-founded · Western heritage, rethought for growing feet · "Designed in Texas. Made with expert footwear partners."

**Avoid unless literally true:** Made in USA · Made in Texas · American-made.

## Core Product Promise

Western style without compromising kids' foot shape.

## Product

Children's western-style cowboy boot with:

- Anatomical toe box
- Low or zero internal drop
- Low toe spring
- Secure heel fit
- Easy on/off (inner (medial) side zip, exposed — no flap)
- Child-safe materials
- Compliance-ready construction and documentation

The product should not feel like a barefoot shoe disguised as a boot. It should feel like a legitimate kids cowboy boot that happens to be built correctly for the shape of a child's foot.

## Target Customer

Design-conscious parents who love the look of western boots but are dissatisfied with the fit, stiffness, narrowness, or raised heel of traditional kids boots.

Likely attributes: parent of a toddler or young child; values quality and aesthetics; will pay more for children's products that feel thoughtful and better designed; may already buy premium or foot-conscious kids shoes; may care about natural foot development, wide toe boxes, Montessori independence, non-toxic materials, heirloom style, outdoor play, or western/Texas identity; does not want orthopedic-looking shoes; wants the boot to photograph well, dress up or down, and hold up to real wear.

## Size Range

**Founding batch (validation run): US kids 8C–12C.** Narrowed from the full range to cut tooling/inventory complexity and simplify the first buy. Accepts some "not in my size" in exchange for a leaner, faster first run.

**Full/expansion range (post-validation): 6C–13C.**

**First prototype sample size: 10C** — large enough to evaluate real walking, on/off, heel security, shaft comfort, and parent appeal, and centered in the founding range. Validate a second size (8C and 12C) before committing the founding run.

## Price Strategy

- Standard retail: **$118**
- Founding batch / preorder: **~$98**
- Headroom to price **above $118** (e.g., $128–$138) if the finished product and landed cost warrant it — do not treat $118 as a ceiling.
- Do not permanently anchor below $100 if the material and design are premium.

The product should be designed to *feel* worth $118+, with early preorder pricing used to create momentum and validate demand.

## Cost Target (internal planning only — not a vendor anchor)

Internal reference: a premium leather small-batch boot of this construction plausibly lands in the **$40–$60+** range before tooling amortization, and the honest number is the *scaled/reorder* cost, not the first run. We will **not** hand developers or factories a fixed landed-cost target; we ask them to cost the product honestly at spec and name the cost drivers and levers. Cost strategy (raise retail vs. simplify non-visible cost vs. accept thin founding-batch margin) is a separate decision to be made once real quotes exist.

## Design Priorities

**Fit priorities (protect the top three most aggressively):**
1. Anatomical toe box
2. Low or zero internal drop
3. Low toe spring
4. Secure heel fit
5. Easy on/off
6. Child-safe materials and compliant testing

Flexible forefoot and flexible shaft are secondary — desirable only insofar as they support comfort and function. They are not the product promise. This is a *real boot that fits*, not a barefoot shoe.

**Aesthetic direction — classic western, softened for children and modern parents.**

The boot reads western through: shaft shape, **pull tabs (kept as a styling cue and light entry assist, not the closure)**, vamp, stitch lines, leather, a **visual heel and heel-breast carved into the outsole profile** (see Technical Fit Brief — achieved without internal heel rise), and western proportions.

The boot avoids: pointed toe, narrow square toe, actual raised heel, costume styling, excessive embroidery, fringe, decorative hardware, removable charms, and anything that introduces compliance, choking, durability, or cost issues.

## MVP Product Definition

One style · one color · one size range · one outsole · one core stitch pattern · one hero product.

- Unisex children's western boot, founding range 8C–12C, sample size 10C
- Warm caramel/camel tan leather
- Short-to-mid shaft height
- **Inner (medial) side zip, exposed (no flap)** as the functional closure; western pull tab(s) retained as styling + assist
- Simple western stitch pattern
- Broad anatomical toe shape (intentionally signals design intent — Altra/Birkenstock-adjacent, softened to read western)
- Low-profile rubber outsole, **zero or near-zero internal drop**, with a visual heel notched into the underside — **slim profile: ≈15 mm total stack as a ceiling, pursue slimmer** (the western heel read comes from notch depth and the heel-breast, not from thickness)
- Low toe spring
- Soft lining; comfortable **removable** insole/sockliner
- No decorative hardware; no complex embroidery for v1

## MVP Color Direction

Warm caramel/camel tan leather — classic western, premium, unisex, easy to style (works with denim, dresses, neutrals, western outfits, everyday kids clothing).

Avoid: orange-heavy tan, yellow-heavy tan, gray beige, overly distressed leather, artificial finishes, cheap shiny coatings.

May be marketed as Caramel, Camel, or Tan depending on the final material; internal target is a warm caramel/camel western leather.

## Material Direction

Leather strongly preferred for MVP — it supports the western story, premium perception, durability, and willingness to pay.

Options to evaluate: full leather upper · corrected-grain leather · soft cowhide · goat leather if cost-effective · leather vamp/foot with a more cost-effective shaft material · suede/nubuck as a later product, not MVP · vegan leather only if it meets the quality, safety, and appearance bar.

The product must not feel plasticky or cheap. At $118+ retail, perceived quality has to be obvious on first touch.

## Last Strategy

**The last is the core product asset, and it is a new anatomical last — never a modified western last.**

We build (or commission) a purpose-made anatomical children's last, or adapt an existing *anatomical* last toward western styling. We do **not** start from a western last and widen it — toe shape, toe spring, and drop *are* the last, and starting western fights the geometry the whole way.

The current 3D model (FreeCAD, built on a downloaded anatomical kids last) is a **concept and communication tool only** — not a production last, and not a licensing basis for tooling. Treat the downloaded last as reference; have the developer build a clean-slate last the company owns outright.

Approach: use the current model to communicate intent → hire developer/last expert → create one 10C sample-size last → prototype in 10C → fit-test on real children → validate 8C and 12C → grade the founding range.

## Manufacturing Philosophy

**Company owns:** product concept, fit philosophy, design direction, aesthetic references, price target, material preference, brand/customer positioning, non-negotiable product standards, **and the last and patterns.**

**Footwear experts support:** last refinement, pattern engineering, tech pack, construction method, material feasibility, grading, prototype execution, compliance documentation, production QC.

Factories are not allowed to redefine the product around their easiest existing last or construction method.

## Product Success Criteria

The MVP succeeds if: parents immediately read it as a real cowboy boot; the toe shape visibly differs from traditional kids western boots; the footbed is zero/near-zero drop; toe spring is minimal; the heel stays secure; kids walk comfortably; parents get it on and off without a fight; the leather feels worth $118+; it photographs beautifully; and customers preorder or join the waitlist at a price that supports healthy margins.

## MVP Avoid List

Do not spend early money on: multiple colors · multiple silhouettes · **cutting the production outsole mold before fit and demand are validated** (tooling is sequenced last, not avoided) · full seasonal line · tall shaft · complex embroidery · exotic leather · wholesale strategy · retail packaging system · expensive brand agency · large production run before demand validation.
