# Technical Fit Brief — v0.3
### Kids Anatomical Western Boot

> Changes from v0.2: primary closure is now a hidden inner side zip (pull-on demoted to a styling/assist detail); the zero-drop heel is specified as a notched-underside outsole geometry; last direction is a new anatomical last (never a modified western last); founding size range narrowed to 8C–12C; removable insole upgraded to standard; sample rounds planned at 4–6.

---

## Purpose

Define the fit and construction priorities for the first children's anatomical western boot. The goal is **not** a generic barefoot boot. The goal is a western-style kids boot that preserves the look of a cowboy boot while correcting the most common fit problems in traditional children's western boots.

## Size Range

- **Founding batch (validation run): US kids 8C–12C.**
- **Full/expansion range (later): 6C–13C.**
- **First prototype sample size: 10C** (centered in the founding range; large enough to test real walking, on/off, heel security, shaft comfort, and parent appeal).
- Validate **8C and 12C** before committing the graded founding run — fitting 10C well does not guarantee the range extremes fit, since toddler and big-kid feet differ in proportion.

## Fit Priority Hierarchy

Develop in this order; protect the top three most aggressively if tradeoffs are required.

1. Anatomical toe box
2. Low or zero internal drop
3. Low toe spring
4. Secure heel fit
5. Easy on/off
6. Child-safe materials and compliance readiness
7. Reasonable comfort/flexibility
8. Western aesthetic fidelity

## Anatomical Toe Box

The toe box must let the child's forefoot and toes sit naturally, not be forced inward by a pointed, snip, narrow-round, or narrow-square western toe.

- Big toe not pushed inward toward the second toe.
- Pinky toe not compressed by a tapered lateral sidewall.
- Visibly broader than conventional children's western boots.
- External silhouette still reads western, not orthopedic.
- Soft western-broad / anatomical shape — **intentionally legible as anatomical** (Altra/Birkenstock-adjacent) to signal the product's reason to exist — but softened so it still reads as a boot, not clownishly round.
- Internal shape matters more than external styling illusion.

**Design note:** the anatomical toe is the *least* western element of the boot, so the shaft, vamp stitching, pull tabs, and heel profile must carry more of the "cowboy" signal than usual. Brief the western read into those elements deliberately.

## Internal Drop

Zero drop, or as close as technically feasible.

Internal drop is the heel-to-forefoot height difference **inside** the boot, not the external side profile. The child's foot should sit level. Any visual western heel must not create meaningful internal heel rise.

- **Preferred target: 0 mm internal drop.**
- Acceptable MVP fallback: near-zero, only if construction or safety requires it, and only after developer review.

## Heel & Drop Geometry (how the visual heel is achieved)

The western heel is created by outsole **geometry**, not by lifting the child's heel. Specified approach:

- A **solid, flat-topped outsole slab** gives a level footbed (true zero internal drop). The top surface the foot sits on is flat heel-to-toe.
- A **notch (undercut) is cut into the *underside* of the slab at the arch**, leaving a forefoot pad and a heel block. Both the forefoot pad and the heel block reach the **same flat ground plane** — so the boot stands level and the foot stays flat.
- The arch (instep) zone is **non-load-bearing**, so removing material there to form the heel breast does not affect drop or the footbed.
- From the side, this reads as a western heel and heel-breast; underfoot, the foot is flat. This is the core trick — **verify in every sample that the slab is not secretly introducing a real heel lift.**

**Constraints this imposes (all confirmed with developer/factory during sampling):**

- **Target ≈15 mm total stack as a ceiling, and pursue slimmer** wherever the heel still reads. The slab must be thick enough to carve a convincing notch **but no thicker than that** — a 20–25 mm stack is too tall and heavy for small feet and is explicitly out of range. The design challenge is a convincing western heel at the *slimmest viable* profile, not a chunky sole.
- **The perceived heel comes from notch depth and a crisp, angled heel-breast — not from raw stack height.** On a small foot, a well-cut ~12–15 mm heel-breast, sharpened with a color/texture break or a stacked-leather-look layering, reads as a real stacked heel. Exhaust these geometric and visual levers before adding any thickness; the floor is set by heel-block durability and the minimum breast height that still reads western (developer to find it).
- To avoid a stiff "plank" underfoot: include **forefoot flex grooves** or a firmer-heel / softer-forefoot durometer split so a child can flex through the step.
- Add a **light shank or sufficient residual midfoot geometry** so the notched arch does not lose torsional stability (no "tacoing" at the midfoot).
- Keep a **slight forefoot bevel at the very toe** for gait roll-off (functional, not exaggerated western toe curl).
- This geometry is **custom** — no stock outsole has a flat-top / notched-underside profile. See tooling note below.

## Toe Spring

Low relative to traditional western boots.

- Avoid exaggerated upward toe lift and aggressive western toe curl.
- Keep only the minimal functional toe spring / forefoot bevel needed for gait roll-off given the outsole stack.
- Measure and document the final toe spring during sampling.

## Heel Fit

Balance anatomical forefoot room with a secure heel — one of the hardest parts of the product, and the reason the closure changed (below).

- **Do not** use a narrow forefoot to hold the foot in place.
- Heel secure enough for walking and play; avoid excessive heel slip.
- Accommodate common toddler/little-kid shapes, including higher insteps and chubbier feet.
- Heel counter provides structure without being stiff or uncomfortable.
- The **hidden side zip is the primary tool for heel security**: it lets the instep/collar close snugly around the foot so the heel seats and the foot doesn't slide forward, without narrowing the toe box. This directly resolves the zero-drop + wide-toe + secure-heel tension that a pull-on can't.

**Key risk retired:** a wide anatomical toe box + zero drop + *pull-on* entry tends to create heel slip. The side zip removes that failure mode; the adult barefoot-western category (Shapen, Koel, etc.) reached the same conclusion.

## Closure & On/Off

**Primary closure: a hidden inner (medial) side zip.**

- Placed on the **instep/medial side** so the outstep (lateral) western profile stays clean for hero photography.
- Opens the boot enough for easy entry over a chubby toddler foot and higher instep, then closes for a snug, secure fit.
- Zip pull and tape must be durable, child-safe, and smooth-operating; no small detachable parts.

**Pull tabs are retained as a western styling cue and a light entry assist** (a functional back/side loop to help seat the foot as the zip closes) — not as the closure mechanism. Durable, easy for small hands/parents to grasp, no detachable parts.

Parent ease is the priority for MVP; child independence is a bonus.

## Shaft

Short-to-mid height for v1.

- Tall enough to read western; short enough to reduce stiffness, cost, material, and on/off difficulty.
- No uncomfortable rub on calf/ankle; holds a clean boot shape without feeling rigid.
- Shaft circumference and height graded by size.
- The shaft carries a meaningful share of the "western" read (see toe note) — brief its shape, stitching, and pull-tab styling deliberately.

## Forefoot Flexibility

Desirable but secondary. The boot should not feel like a rigid miniature adult boot, but should not chase extreme barefoot flexibility at the expense of looking and wearing like a boot. Flex grooves / durometer split (above) deliver "reasonable" not "barefoot-thin."

## Outsole

- Low-profile rubber, durable for outdoor play, flexible enough for children's walking.
- Zero / near-zero **internal** drop via the flat-top / notched-underside geometry above.
- Western-inspired side profile via the heel notch and breast.
- Must **not**: create meaningful internal heel elevation · add aggressive toe spring · feel heavy · read as a sneaker sole (unless intentionally styled).

**Tooling note:** this outsole is custom. To keep tooling out of the validation budget, ask the developer and factories whether the **founding batch can use a CNC- or die-cut notched outsole cut from rubber sheet and hand-finished**, accepting a higher unit cost to **defer the mold** until reorder volume justifies it. Cutting the production mold is sequenced as the *last* major spend, after fit and demand are validated.

## Upper Material

- Leather or leather-forward. MVP color: warm caramel/camel tan.
- Soft enough for kids, durable enough for play, premium handfeel, safe for child testing.
- No finishes, dyes, or coatings that create compliance risk.
- Must support a $118+ retail perception.

Cost-saving options to evaluate (without cheapening the visible/structural product): corrected-grain leather · soft cowhide · goat leather if cost-effective · leather vamp/foot with a cost-efficient shaft material · simpler stitch pattern · shared material across sizes/colorways.

## Color Target

Warm caramel/camel tan — classic western, premium, unisex, easy to style; warm but not orange, rich but not dark brown.

Avoid: yellow tan · gray beige · orange-heavy cognac · overly distressed leather · cheap shiny finishes.

## Lining & Insole

- Comfortable against foot and sock; child-safe; breathable where feasible.
- Must not create unintended heel rise or narrow the usable toe box.
- **Removable insole is standard** — it's a cheap fit-tuning lever, extends size life (pull it for more toe room as the foot grows), and gives clean control of the zero-drop footbed. Confirm it doesn't create a compliance/durability issue.

## Compliance & Safety (budgeted workstream, not a checklist)

Develop as a children's product from day one. Budget real dollars and lab lead-time.

- No detachable decorative parts; no small hardware for MVP.
- Child-safe leather, dyes, finishes, adhesives, trims — **leather tanning/dyes are a real lead-and-chromium area** and can constrain the caramel target; vet early.
- Tracking-label plan; Children's Product Certificate (CPC) plan.
- Material/component documentation from the factory; third-party testing on production-representative samples before shipment.
- Clear assignment of responsibility across brand, manufacturer, importer, and testing lab.

## Fit-Test Plan

Test the prototype on children with different foot shapes: average-width · wider/chubbier toddler foot · narrow heel · higher instep · current barefoot/foot-shaped wearer · current traditional-cowboy-boot wearer · plus a style-first parent and a foot-health-first parent.

Questions: Can the parent get it on easily (zip operation)? Can the child walk naturally? Heel slip? Enough toe room? Foot sliding forward? Shaft rub? Does it look like a real cowboy boot? Does the material feel worth $118+? Would the parent pay $118 / preorder at $98? What size confusion or exchange risk appears?

## Prototype Development Plan

Plan for **4–6 sample rounds** (not 3) — a novel anatomical last threading toe/drop/heel-security rarely resolves in three.

- **Round 1:** confirm silhouette, last direction, toe shape, shaft height, material feel, notched-outsole feasibility, zip placement/operation.
- **Rounds 2–3:** correct heel security, toe box, zip/entry, drop verification, toe spring, material feel.
- **Rounds 4–6 (as needed):** dial fit across 8C/10C/12C, confirm pre-production sample for photography, preorder, costing, and compliance.

**Do not proceed to full production** until the last, heel fit, toe shape, zip/on-off, material quality, verified zero/near-zero drop, and perceived value are validated.
